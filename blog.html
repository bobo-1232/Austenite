One of my goals this year is to read all the books that I'd read when I was 15. I remember reading Catcher in the Rye three times, and my perception of the main character Holden Caulfield changed each time I reread the book. When I was 15, I thought Holden was mature, brave, and humorous, ignoring Holden was a lost, depressed teenager who couldn't understand his feelings. When I was 17, I realized that Holden didn't find the right way to cope with his depression, and he tried every way to escape from his problems. He was naive and anxious and needed help from adults. I became empathetic toward him when I reread the book. The book always reminded me of a piece of my adolescence.
